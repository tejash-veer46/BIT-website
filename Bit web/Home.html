CURRENT WATER CONDITION

In Jharkhand entire population does not have access to safe drinking water & sanitation. Rural sanitation coverage is very low. About 30% of habitations have partial facility with drinking water. All habitations do not have safe drinking water as source contains Fluoride, Arsenic & Iron.
The programs such as Accelerated Rural Water Supply Programme (ARWSP), Swajaldhara and Total Sanitation Campaign were launched in serving the rural population with water and sanitation related services all across the State.

Jharkhand is a mineral rich state and there is ample scope for industrialisation and for industrialisation there is a significant amount of water requirement. Considering the fact that water is a scarce commodity in Jharkhand a balanced allocation of water to different sectors is required.

JHARKHAND 

Jharkhand was founded on 15th Nov 2000 and is famous for its rich mineral resources like Uranium, Mica, Bauxite, Granite, Gold, Silver, Graphite, Magnetite, Dolomite etc.

A small overview

Major portion of the Jharkhand state lies on the Chota Nagpur Plateau and the plateau consisted of Chotanagpur Granite Gneissic Complex (CGGC). Granite-gneiss, schist, phyllite and other rocks belonging to CGGC which covered almost 85% of the geographical area of the state.

In Summer (May 2022), about 67.3% of the total water level throughout the state varied in the range of 5-10 mbgl, 18.5% of the total water level varied in the range of 2-5 mbgl, 1.8% of the total water level were 10 mbgl .
